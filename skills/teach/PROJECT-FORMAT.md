# PROJECT.md Format

Use `PROJECT.md` when a real, multi-session deliverable or experiment will organize learning better than disconnected lessons. Keep one primary project per teaching workspace; use checkpoints rather than parallel project sprawl.

## Template

```md
# Project: {Observable deliverable}

## Outcome
{What the user will produce or be able to decide, and who will use it.}

## Baseline
{Current artifact, diagnostic result, or demonstrated capability. Separate observed evidence from self-assessment.}

## Deliverables
- {Concrete artifact or capability}
- {Another artifact or capability}

## Guardrails And Non-goals
- {Safety, budget, data, time, or scope boundary}

## Evidence Contract
- Inputs and source:
- Data or knowledge cutoff:
- Training/practice boundary:
- Validation or transfer boundary:
- Success metric:
- Failure or cancellation condition:

## Checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1: {Capability}
- Exercise:
- Observable pass condition:
- Artifact:
- Status: pending | in-progress | demonstrated | revise

## Experiment Log

### Experiment 1: {Name}
- Hypothesis:
- Inputs and cutoff:
- Method:
- Metric:
- Result:
- Interpretation:
- What this does not prove:
- Next action:
```

## Rules

- Make the outcome useful outside the lesson while keeping practice safe and reversible.
- Record exact data/source dates when results can drift.
- Never use training or worked-example performance as validation evidence.
- Do not mark a checkpoint `demonstrated` until the learner produces evidence against its pass condition.
- Revise the project when the mission changes; do not keep stale checkpoints alive for continuity's sake.
